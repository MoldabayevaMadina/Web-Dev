Travel to Beijing, China

  • Food to eat

Places to visit

  • Forbidden City (Palace Museum):
    Start your journey at one of Beijing’s most iconic landmarks. The Forbidden City is an expansive palace complex that was the home of Chinese emperors for over 500 years. Explore the vast courtyards, grand halls, and museums within the walls, learning about China’s royal history
  • Mausoleum and The National Museum of China.
    It’s an important historical and cultural site in the heart of Beijing.

Food to eat

  • Peking Duck.
    It is Beijing's most famous dish, known for its crispy skin and tender meat, typically served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, and fresh vegetables. The most famous restaurants for Peking Duck are Quanjude and Da Dong.
  • Beijing Noodles:
    Zhajiangmian (fried sauce noodles) is a must-try. It’s a dish of noodles topped with a thick, savory soy-bean paste sauce, minced pork, and fresh vegetables. You’ll find this dish at many local restaurants.
